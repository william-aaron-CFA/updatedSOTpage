1505 At optical wavelengths Hickson Compact Group 90 exhibits an unprecedented amount of diffuse intracluster light whose total luminosity is approximately 40% of the group luminosity. Three luminous galaxies, including the hard X-ray source NGC 7172, are currently strongly interacting and merging, producing a substantial amount of tidal debris which comprises the intracluster light. Previous ROSAT HRI observations of HCG 90 hint at the presence of weak, diffuse X-ray emission. We propose to use the AXAF ACIS detector to acquire better data to confirm that HCG 90 has an extended region of X-ray emission with very low value of L_x/L_opt indicating that the ICM in this group is just now in the process of forming and heating as a direct response to strong galaxy-galaxy interactions.
