3133 We propose to use Cyg X-2 as a bright 'candle' behind a sufficient dense column of interstellar matter in order to study absorption and scattering effects. Energy resolved scattering halos provide a clue to the chemistry of interstellar dust including the question if grains have a Silicate core and an organic mantle. Since haloes are not completely observable with a dispersive instrument, we will use the brightness distribution in cross dispersion direction for measuring the energy dependent scattering cross section. Cyg X-2 is the brightest among reasonably absorbed sources (NH > 10**21 cm**-2). We expect about 18 cts/s with HRC-S + LETG more than 500.000 cts within a 30ksec observation, respectively.
