2425 Observing X-ray shadows produced by neutral gas is the most direct way to decompose the soft X-ray background along a line of sight, therefore to tighten fundamental limits on various X-ray source populations and hot gas in the universe. Well-suited for such a shadowing experiment is the gas-rich, edge-on spiral NGC 4244, which is located in a direction of low Galactic X-ray absorption and is exceptionally inactive in star formation. The proposed ACIS observation of the galaxy will enable us to decompose the soft X-ray background in the 0.2-1 keV range into two separate components: Galactic and extragalactic. This decomposition is particularly important because it may provide a unique diagnosis of the thermal and chemical properties of the hot intergalactic medium.
